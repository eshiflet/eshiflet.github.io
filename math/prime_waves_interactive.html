Prime Waves

Each wave is cos(2πx/n). A wave peaks at 1 exactly when n divides x. Add more waves (more factors n tested, up to 1000) and watch which numbers stop "looking prime." The x-axis range grows automatically so the largest wave's full period stays in view.
